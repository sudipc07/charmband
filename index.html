The field reality

Why existing solutions don't work in the field.

01

The smartphone app problem.

Most janitorial workers in India don't carry smartphones. Even when they do, they don't remember to start and stop sessions through a 9-hour shift. Workforce-management apps routinely miss 30-40% of sessions, even in white-collar environments with formal HR oversight.

02

The QR code problem.

Every QR-based check-in has the same failure mode: someone prints the QR and tapes it to the supervisor's desk. A dozen workers tap once and walk off. The dashboard shows full coverage; the contractor pays for work that never happened; the client gets a false report.

03

The supervisor honesty problem.

Manual logs assume supervisors mark workers present when present and absent when absent. In an industry with high attrition and per-head payment pressure, that assumption quietly breaks every shift.

The CHARM approach

Evidence that doesn't depend on what anyone remembers to do.

The CHARM Band is a sensor-grade wristband worn by the worker through their shift. Embedded motion sensors continuously recognise activity patterns: walking, mopping, vacuuming, scrubbing, standing idle.

The band is paired to the worker at shift start and returned at shift end. The output is a complete narrative of where the worker was, what they were doing, and for how long.

There is no app for the worker. Nothing to start. Nothing to stop. Nothing to remember.

Three steps

From shift start to verifiable record.

  1. 1

    Pair.

    Worker taps their ID card at the shift station and is issued a CHARM Band for the day.

  2. 2

    Wear.

    Worker performs the shift as normal. The band recognises activity and zone presence in the background.

  3. 3

    Return.

    At shift end, the band returns to the station. The full shift narrative is ready for review.
